Workout Tips

The tips we provide serve as an excellent foundation for pursuing your personal fitness goals. Whether you're preparing for your first powerlifting meet, aiming to cut for a bodybuilding or bikini competition, or simply looking to slim down for summer, everyone's path begins at a different place. To maximize your progress, consider scheduling a one-on-one session. Together, we can craft a mesocycle tailored precisely to your unique objectives.

Stay hydrated, warm up before you lift, and always focus on form over weight.

Nutrition Notes

Protein post-workout is essential. Consider lean meats or plant-based shakes within 30 minutes.

Calculate Macros

Daily Macros Are Calculated By:
Determining Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)
This is the number of calories your body needs at rest. Common formulas:

Mifflin-St Jeor is widely used:
For men: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age) + 5
For women: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age) − 161

Adjusting for Activity Level
Multiply BMR by an activity multiplier to get TDEE:

Sedentary: ×1.2
Lightly active: ×1.375
Moderately active: ×1.55
Very active: ×1.725
Extra active: ×1.9

Setting a Caloric Goal
For fat loss: TDEE − 10–20%
For maintenance: TDEE
For muscle gain: TDEE + 10–20%

Splitting into Macronutrients
A common macro ratio for general fitness might be:
Carbs: 45–55%
Protein: 25–35%
Fats: 20–30%

Converting Grams from Calories
Carbs: 4 calories per gram
Protein: 4 calories per gram
Fat: 9 calories per gram
